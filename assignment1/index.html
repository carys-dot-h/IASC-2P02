1. Flatness

From inside the cave...

Beyond the sea, in a rocky coastal cove lined with sand and stone that has been carefully polished by the lapping of the waves, there lies a cave. Its inhabitants are born within, and will die within, spending their entire lives within the cave. To them, there is no outside world or even a need to see if there is an outside world because everything has been explained to them through the dark images that dance along the wall. These images are thin, coalesced with splotches that connect and sway amongst each other, dazzling in their movement. Sometimes the splotches blur together, dappling upon the cave wall in varying, translucent and opaque waves, resulting in cries of wonder and joy from those who live there. They watch as the darkness sways gently, soothingly, and feel comforted. Then, the movement shudders violently, and part of the image shatters. Now, they watch in horror as the thin shape and its connected blotches fall, still connected but fractured. Their source of comfort has turned sour. A wail starts up from the back of the group, and others follow suit, the inhabitants trembling with a fearful certainty.

At the beginning, they were told what the movement meant, and that is how they have conducted their lives ever since, generation after generation. The slow swaying of the shapes is soothing to them, as they were told that it indicated all was well, long ago. That gentle sway has soothed children and elders alike. A faster movement, they were told, meant trouble had a potential of finding its way to them, that the gods were unhappy. It meant that there was a possibility of a break in the pattern, the forces of chaos making its mark and impacting their livelihoods. It was an ill omen to witness a break, a discordant harmony in the blotches that danced across the cave wall. They cry out in fear for they know that the gods are angry, and nothing else beside that. They only know the certainty of the cave and the images. Their home is more so of a prison, but they have long since become complacent with the way their lives are. For they know nothing but the cave, and are content within it, so why would they wish for anything else? Why would they think to look beyond the certainty of their existence that brings them the sense of calmness and joy that nothing else possibly could?

2. Seeing Double

From outside the cave...

Sometimes, noises could be heard from the cave on the beach. Those wandering by would look curiously at it before continuing on their way. Some say it is haunted, others say that the noises coming from within were only the result of the wind tunnelling through the rough stone. Near the sandy shore and the rocky, outcropped entrance to the cave there stands a tree. The tree is old, weathered, but holds fast against the stream of time. When the weather turned for the worse, its bark would creak and groan as its branches moved faster with the wind and rain.. Storms were common for the region, and those who lived there had learned to weather them well, barely batting an eye when another one rolled in on the eastern winds. On particularly bad days, the power of the wind would force parts of the old tree to crack, splinter, and break. Sometimes the branches barely held onto their tethers, other times they fell loosely to the ground, the fall cushioned by the abundance of leaves that the branch bore. The chances of it completely falling depended on the strength of the branch itself, as nature is, understandably, governed by the law of the fittest. That which is strong prospers.

Those outside the cave do not fear these happenings. For they know how unpredictable a force the weather can be and how it impacts the natural world around them. This knowledge was passed down from their ancestors, so long ago now that it isn’t knowledge to them at all, it is just common sense combined with natural science and understanding. Over generations they have learned the intricacies of how weather works, how they can tell if a storm is on the horizon or not (as opposed to the cave’s inhabitants, who do not know of weather or nature). They know that they only have something to fear if the storm is truly violent, strong enough to knock down old trees with extensive roots such as the one on the beach. It has been there for generations and yet remains healthy. It is nourished from the sea and the near-constant presence of the sun. There is no doubt in the minds of those outside the cave that the tree will remain for generations more to come, just as the noises of the cave will also seemingly continue. At least, unless someone outside of the cave chooses to let curiosity take flight, to see for themselves what lies within instead of continuing their usual lives.
