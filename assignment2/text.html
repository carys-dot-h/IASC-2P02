The past of Middle-Earth is explained to the audience through the forging of the rings of power by Sauron. Sauron’s master ring, the One Ring, was forged in secret in Mount Doom, and was used to bend Middle-Earth to his will. An alliance of men and elves was created to defeat Sauron, and the ring was taken from him, defeating him. It was claimed by one of the victors until it betrayed him, and afterwards the ring was found by Gollum. Later, the ring is taken from Gollum by a hobbit. Our story truly begins in the Shire, a peaceful country whose inhabitants are the hobbits. A hobbit by the name of Bilbo Baggins is celebrating his birthday. His nephew and fellow hobbit, Frodo Baggins, waits in the woods for the wizard Gandalf to arrive for the celebrations. The two greet each other as friends, and Gandalf heads up to Bilbo’s hobbit hole to say that he’s arrived. At the party, we are introduced to three other hobbits of importance to the story - Samwise Gamgee (Sam), Meriadoc Brandybuck (Merry), and Peregrin Took (Pippin). Merry and Pippin play a prank, but are caught by Gandalf. Bilbo gives a speech at the end of the evening which starts out pleasant, but soon confuses the other partygoers. Bilbo says goodbye to the Shire and then vanishes into thin air! It is revealed that Bilbo used the magic ring he had found in Gollum’s cave to turn invisible and properly leave the Shire. Gandalf confronts him and chastises him for his prank, telling him to leave the ring with Frodo. Eventually, Bilbo agrees and leaves the ring behind for Frodo to inherit, and Gandalf tells Frodo to keep it secret and safe before leaving as well. Gandalf travels to Gondor where he reads the account of the survivor who originally took the ring from Sauron. The description he gives matches the image of the ring Frodo now has. He rushes back to the Shire to confirm his suspicions, revealing the inscription written upon the ring. Gandalf tells Frodo that he needs to leave immediately, to make his way to the Prancing Pony in Bree, and that he will meet him there. They hear a noise from outside, revealing that Sam has been eavesdropping. Gandalf tasks Sam to travel with Frodo. Gandalf goes to another wizard, Saruman, to tell him the ring has been found. However, Saruman has already betrayed Gandalf for Sauron, and informs him that the Nazgul are already on their way to the Shire. He then imprisons Gandalf on top of his tower. Frodo and Sam travel through the Shire, and they meet Merry and Pippin along the way. On the road, they are forced to hide from the Nazgul, narrowly escaping their grasp as Frodo is tempted to put on the ring. They arrive at the Prancing Pony, but Gandalf is not there to meet them. An incident in the tavern ends with Frodo slipping the ring on his finger. The Nazgul are immediately drawn to the ring and Frodo takes it off. He is then cornered by a man named Strider, who the others don’t trust. Strider leads them into the wilderness, telling the hobbits that he is taking them to Rivendell, an elven settlement. He leaves them atop an old ruin one night while he scouts the area, but the hobbits light a fire while Frodo is sleeping and draw the Nazgul to their location. In the ensuing fight, Frodo is tempted by the ring again, putting it on. While it’s on, one of the Nazgul stabs him with a Morgul blade, causing him to take it off. Strider arrives just in time to save them. Gandalf witnesses Saruman building an army of hybrid orcs from atop the tower of Isengard. Frodo’s injury is severe, and Strider looks for herbs to cure him. An elf named Arwen arrives on horseback and says that she will take Frodo the rest of the way to Rivendell. The Nazgul give chase, but are no match for elvish magic. Frodo wakes up after being healed by Elrond, Arwen’s father, and meets with Gandalf, who escaped captivity. He and the other hobbits are prepared to go home, thinking their job is finished. He also reunites with Bilbo. Arwen and Strider, whose true name is Aragorn, talk about his ancestor, Isildur, the man who took the ring for himself. The elf pledges her love to Aragorn. Representatives from distant lands arrive to discuss the fate of the ring. At the meeting, a Fellowship is formed as Frodo volunteers to destroy it. The Fellowship consists of the four hobbits, Aragorn, Gandalf, a dwarf named Gimli, an elf named Legolas, and a man named Boromir. The Fellowship leaves Rivendell but are soon forced to change their travel route because of Saruman’s interference. On the way, the ring tempts Boromir. It is decided that they will travel through the Mines of Moria. They come to the gates of Moria and answer a riddle to open them, but the entrance is blocked by a monster once they enter. It is soon revealed that Gollum is following the Fellowship, wanting to be reunited with the ring. The dwarves who tried to reclaim Moria are all dead, much to Gimli’s grief. Pippin alerts the goblins of Moria to their presence, and a fight ensues. When they are surrounded by goblins, something else makes its way to them, causing the goblins to flee. The Balrog approaches and Gandalf tells the Fellowship to run. He confronts it on the Last Bridge, seemingly defeating it before being dragged down into the depths of Moria by the creature’s whip. The Fellowship grieves the loss of Gandalf. Aragorn leads them to the nearby elven kingdom of Lothlorien. They are greeted by Galadriel, who offers them respite and gifts to help them on their journey. While in Lothlorien, Frodo and Galadriel talk, and Frodo understands the task ahead of him. They leave Lothlorien whilst being tracked by the hybrid orcs Saruman created: the Uruk-hai. Soon, Boromir gives into the temptation of the ring and attempts to take it from Frodo. Frodo puts on the ring and flees, deciding that he must complete his task on his own. The Uruk-hai catch up, attacking the Fellowship, who fight them off while Frodo escapes. Boromir dies defending Merry and Pippin, who are taken by the Uruk-hai to be delivered to Saruman. Aragorn comes to his rescue too late. Frodo takes one of the elven boats and begins to cross the river, but Sam refuses to let him go alone. The two hobbits decide to travel to Mordor together. Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli let them leave, setting off in pursuit of the Uruk-hai that took Merry and Pippin after giving Boromir a funeral. Frodo and Sam begin their walk to Mordor, and the story ends.
